# Competitive Landscape
Keep a current picture of your competitors — moves, launches, gaps, and the implications for your roadmap.

## What it does
Researches your competitive set across the open web, competitive-intelligence tools, and your own internal notes, then organizes it into a scannable landscape: who each competitor is, what they've shipped recently, how they position, and where the gaps and threats are for you. It ends with implications — what these moves mean for your product decisions.

## Connectors it uses
| Category | Placeholder | What it adds |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Competitive intelligence | `~~competitive intelligence` | Traffic, market-share, and tracked-competitor signals to ground claims in data |
| Knowledge base | `~~knowledge base` | Your prior battlecards, win/loss notes, and positioning docs for continuity |

> **No connectors?** The skill runs on web research alone — recent launches, pricing pages, and news — and folds in anything you paste. Connectors add hard numbers and your internal history.

1. Define the **competitive set** and the product area in scope.

4. Identify **positioning gaps** — where you win, where you're exposed.

5. Draw out **implications** for roadmap and messaging.

> **Sanity-check before you circulate.** Competitive claims can be wrong or stale; verify anything decision-critical against a primary source before sharing externally.
